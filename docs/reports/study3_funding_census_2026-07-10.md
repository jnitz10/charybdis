## Persistence classification

The pre-registered bar is rank rho ≥ 0.5 and shock half-life ≥ 24 hours. The half-life leg is STRUCTURALLY UNREACHABLE at hourly funding cadence in this census: a 24-hour half-life requires phi ≥ 0.9715, while the maximum observed phi is 0.933. The observed maximum half-life is 9.9 hours and the median is 1.3 hours. Consequently, the AND-gate is always false as gated (0 of 183 markets), and `carry_relevant` is uninformative at this cadence.

Shock-half-life distribution across the 181 markets with a defined stationary half-life: minimum 0.2h; p10 0.6h; p25 0.9h; median 1.3h; p75 2.1h; p90 2.7h; maximum 9.9h. HIP-3 funding is strongly persistent hour-to-hour (phi up to 0.93) but mean-reverts within approximately 1–10h. This is not "carry is dead"; it is a cadence-versus-bar mismatch.

Tri-state evidence counts, carry / measured_fail / insufficient: 0 / 169 / 14. `carry_relevant` remains the backward-compatible boolean and is true only for the `carry` state.

The half-life bar is a pre-registered operator OVERRIDE POINT: an operator could choose to lower it. This statement records the pre-registered decision point and does not alter the reported gate.

Per-market rank persistence is the lag-1 Spearman correlation of that market's Monday-start weekly cross-sectional rank series. At least four weekly observations are required for this leg to count as evidence. The census also stores the mean and median adjacent-week cross-sectional Spearman correlations across shared markets.

## Frozen T3 universe

The HIP-3-only universe applies the strict day-volume floor of USD 1,000,000; main-dex BTC/ETH/SOL reference rows remain in the census but are not T3 universe rows.

S-C (T3) selects its universe by the USD 1,000,000 liquidity floor plus coverage, not by `carry_relevant` (which is empty). The frozen-universe contract therefore does not depend on passing the carry gate.

## Biases and assumptions

- Snapshot OI and day volume are current cross-sectional size proxies joined by exact market key; they are not historical executable depth.
- OI notional uses the snapshot oracle price. APR × size products can be negative and do not model slippage, borrow, fees, or liquidation constraints.
- AR(1) uses OLS with an intercept on adjacent hourly settlements. AR(1), AC(1), and AC(24) pairs exclude lag windows that cross an inter-settlement gap longer than 1.5 hours. Half-life is null unless the fitted coefficient is strictly between zero and one.
- Regime runs use strict APR thresholds and break on an inter-settlement gap longer than 1.5 hours. No funding row is shifted backward to the premium-formation hour.
- Weekly ranks use partial inception/final weeks when present; differing market inception dates reduce rank-series observations for newer markets.
- Candle truncation does not remove funding observations. No-data markets have no census statistic; funding-truncated status is retained explicitly when present.
